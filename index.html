001 — About the Course

Multimedia
Authoring.

The MMA course fuses motion design, 3D modeling, compositing and traditional animation into a unified creative practice. Students work with Blender, After Effects and Procreate — bridging historical technique with modern digital pipelines.

The core deliverable is a 60-second rotoscoped animation built at ~5 seconds per week, culminating in a fully composited final film with sound, colour, and 3D backgrounds.

✏️ What is Rotoscoping?
Frame-by-frame tracing of live-action footage to produce fluid animation. Patented by Max Fleischer in 1917, it teaches real weight, timing and rhythm that elevate all other work.
🪐 The 3D Connection
3D rendering in Blender runs parallel to rotoscoping. The solar system project challenges mastery of lighting, texturing and shading — skills that directly inform compositing.
📽️ The 60-Second Film
Over 14 weeks, each student builds a 60-second rotoscoped animation — roughly 5 new seconds each week. This portfolio documents every step of that journey.
